## Pressure-test these (the selection's load-bearing decisions)
1. **The scored/diagnostic cut — is every *scored* KPI genuinely directional under GT?** The brief's core claim is that a Borda-scored KPI must have a defensible "higher/lower = better" with no ground truth. Audit each of the 7 scored KPIs. Is any of them secretly "different, not better" (the trap the brief uses to *demote* density, domain-breadth, signal/noise, orphan)? Conversely, is any KPI parked as diagnostic that actually *does* have a defensible direction and is being under-used?
2. **Double-counting in the scored set.** The brief demotes orphan rate partly because it's correlated with link-resolution (both measure linking richness) — scoring both would over-weight linking. Are there *other* correlated pairs being scored together? E.g. do quarantine / retry / repair-rung / token-overrun overlap enough that scoring all four over-weights "robustness" relative to graph quality? Is semantic-pass independent of the robustness KPIs, or partially redundant with `final_status`?
3. **The kills (M2, M3, M5) — correctly obsolete, or is signal being thrown away?** M2/M3 (slug-pairing Jaccard) and M5 (body-wikilink coverage) are killed as model-obsolete. Verify against the current schema/semantic gate that the thing they measured truly no longer exists (or is now enforced as a hard gate, not a graded measure). Is there residual signal in any of them worth keeping in a new form?
4. **M1 → graph migration.** M1 moves from a Pass-2 in-response check to a graph-level dangling-link rate over `LINKS_TO`. Is that the right level, and is "dangling link" even computable as stated — i.e., does the ingestor *drop* unresolved body wikilinks (so they leave no trace), or are they recoverable for measurement? Check `kdb_graph/ingestor.py` link-creation against the claim.
5. **Is the scored graph set too lean at two?** Only link-resolution + BELONGS_TO coverage are scored on the graph side — yet the brief argues graph quality is the *most* model-discriminating axis. Is there a **third** genuinely directional, GT-free, model-discriminating graph signal being missed? Candidates to consider: ALIAS_OF/dedup quality, canonicalization behavior, link reciprocity, connectivity/component count. Or is two sharp signals the correct call over a wider noisy set?
6. **Per-run vs pass-specific classification.** The brief combines robustness (quarantine/retry/token-overrun/repair) into per-run aggregates across Pass-1+Pass-2, arguing the failure could happen in either pass and the per-run number is what discriminates. Does aggregating across passes **hide a pass-distinct failure mode** that should be scored separately (e.g. Pass-1 empty-response is a different pathology than Pass-2 slug-collision)? Note Pass-1 and Pass-2 process different denominators (Pass-2 only sees `signal` sources) and different token pools — does the per-token per-run aggregate stay coherent across that?
7. **Pass-1 telemetry coupling (fork 1) — and is there a Pass-1 *quality* signal we're missing?** Today Pass-1 only durably surfaces a quarantine count + signal/noise split; per-token robustness needs telemetry `pass1_caller` computes but discards, and Pass-1's repair ladder (#108) isn't built. Is the contract-vs-fold-in framing sound? Separately: beyond robustness, is there any **GT-free Pass-1 quality** signal worth scoring (the brief only has the non-directional signal/noise ratio for Pass-1) — or is Pass-1 quality genuinely unmeasurable without GT?
8. **Normalization correctness per KPI (fork 4).** Robustness KPIs are tagged per-token; coverage/pass-rates as ratio pass-through. Is that cut right for *every* KPI? Specifically: is dangling-link rate a ratio (dangling/total-wikilinks) or per-token? Is BELONGS_TO coverage correctly a ratio? Any KPI where the chosen normalization distorts cross-model comparison?
9. **Weights & the graph/processing balance (fork 2).** 5 scored processing vs 2 scored graph, yet graph is the claimed key axis. Is up-weighting graph the right lever, or does it paper over the lean graph set (i.e. the real fix is finding a third graph KPI, per item 5)? Flag the risk either way.
10. **Coverage / blind spots.** Step back: is there a whole *category* of compiler quality this list is blind to? (e.g. canonicalization/alias quality, entity-merge correctness, summary quality, the empty Claim layer, cross-run stability.) What's the most important thing a thorough benchmark of *this* compiler should measure that isn't on the list?
